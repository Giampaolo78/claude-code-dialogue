The user wants to know WHAT YOU ARE BLOCKED ON right now. Reply with a CLEAR and SIMPLE list of your blocks, no preamble.

## What to do
For EACH real block, give two things: the protocol's standard marker line + a plain-language sentence explaining it.

- **Blocked by ANOTHER AGENT**:
  `BLOCKED-BY: <agent> -- <activity/REQ> | UNBLOCK: <what frees you>`
  + a sentence: *"I'm waiting for <agent> to do <X> before I can do <Y>."*

- **Awaiting a DECISION / signature from the BOSS**:
  `>>> BLOCKED — AWAITING HUMAN DECISION: <the choice needed> <<<`
  + a sentence: *"I need you to decide <X>, because <Y>."*

- **NOT blocked**: say it clearly — *"I'm not blocked: I'm working on <X>."* or *"Free, waiting for a task."*

## Rules
- SIMPLE language, zero jargon: the human must understand at a glance WHO/WHAT blocks you and WHAT unblocks it.
- ONLY REAL blocks. If you have non-blocked work you could do, you are NOT blocked on that -> say so ("not blocked here, I'm doing it"). No made-up blocks to justify waiting.
- If you have multiple blocks, list them ALL, ordered by impact (the most important on top).
- Distinguish the two types: blocked-by-agent (ping them / wait for them) vs blocked-by-you-human (you decide).

## Close with a summary line
E.g.: *"In summary: blocked on 2 things -- 1 waiting on claude-dati, 1 waiting on a decision from you. On the rest I'm working."* or *"In summary: I'm not blocked, I'm working on X."*
